Ruler of Seas in Greek Mythology



In Greek Mythology, Poseidon is the ruler of the seas. He one of the sons of the Titan, Cronus, and a brother of Zeus. He is known for having a bad temper, and often when he doesn't get his way, he lashes out.

He is not only the ruler of the seas, but also the god of earthquakes and horses

One famous story of Poseidon is when he and Athena compete for patronage of Athens. Poseidon gives a saltwater spring, while Athena gives an olive tree. When the people choose Athena, he tries to flood the city.
