Ruler of Seas in Roman Mythology

In Roman Mythology, Neptune is the ruler of the seas. He is the son of Saturn, and a brother of Jupiter. Like Poseidon, he is known to have a bad temper, and to wield a trident.

Like Poseidon, Neptune is the ruler of the seas, earthquakes, and horses; but he is also the god of freshwater bodies.

One famous story of Neptune is when Aeneas, the founder of Rome, is being attacked by the goddess Juno, and Neptune sees Aeneas's importance and protects him.
